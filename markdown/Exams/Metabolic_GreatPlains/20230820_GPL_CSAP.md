## Abnormal Findings Summary

### Critical Findings

1. **Lactobacillus family: No Growth (NG)**
   - Lactobacillus is a key beneficial bacteria that should be present
   - Absence indicates insufficiency dysbiosis
   - Contributes to maintaining acidic intestinal pH and protecting against pathogens

2. **Citrobacter freundii complex: 3+ (Dysbiotic)**
   - A gram-negative bacterium that is dysbiotic at 3+ or greater
   - Can cause diarrheal disease, GI infection and inflammation
   - Produces E. coli-like heat-stable enterotoxin and hydrogen sulfide
   - Thrives on fructooligosaccharides (FOS) - common in artificial sweeteners

3. **Lysozyme: 800 ng/mL (High)**
   - Reference: ≤ 500 ng/mL
   - Elevated lysozyme indicates inflammatory immune response in the gut
   - Commonly associated with significant overgrowth of enteropathogens
   - May be remediated by eliminating offending microorganisms and using anti-inflammatory nutraceuticals

4. **pH: 5.5 (Low)**
   - Reference: 5.8 – 7.0
   - More acidic than expected
   - Associated with rapid transit time (diarrhea or loose stools)
   - May indicate lactose malabsorption/intolerance
   - Unabsorbed lactose can be hydrolyzed by colonic bacteria forming volatile fatty acids

## Report Recommendations

### For Lactobacillus Deficiency:
- Supplementation with probiotics containing Lactobacillus strains
- Consumption of fermented foods (yogurt, kefir, miso, tempeh, tamari sauce)
- Soluble fiber and polyphenols (chocolate, green tea, blackcurrant, red wine, grape seed extracts) to increase beneficial bacteria

### For Citrobacter freundii:
- Avoid fructooligosaccharides (FOS) found in artificial/alternative sweeteners
- Natural agents with high sensitivity: Olive Leaf Extract, Oregano, Ionic Silver, Colloidal Silver
- If symptoms are prolonged, antibiotics may be indicated: Ceftazidime, Ciprofloxacin, or Sulfamethoxazole/Trimethoprim are susceptible

### For Elevated Lysozyme:
- Elimination of offending enteroinvasive microorganisms (Citrobacter)
- Use of anti-inflammatory nutraceuticals: quercetin, vitamin C, curcumin, gamma-linoleic acid, omega-3 fatty acids (EPA, DHA), aloe vera

### For Low pH:
- Investigate cause of rapid transit (food intolerance, infection)
- Consider lactose malabsorption testing
- Address underlying dysbiosis

### General GI Support:
- Nutrients for GI mucosa regeneration: zinc, beta-carotene, pantothenic acid, L-glutamine
