## Report Interpretations and Recommendations

### High Yeast/Fungal Metabolites (Tartaric, Arabinose)
Elevations of these metabolites indicate yeast/fungal overgrowth of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract. 

**Recommendations:**
- Prescription or natural (botanical) anti-fungals
- Supplementation with high potency multi-strain probiotics

### High Hippuric Acid
May derive from food, GI bacterial activity, or exposure to the solvent toluene. Most hippuric acid in urine is derived from microbial breakdown of chlorogenic acid to benzoic acid. Chlorogenic acid is common in beverages and many fruits and vegetables including apples, pears, tea, coffee, sunflower seeds, carrots, blueberries, cherries, potatoes, tomatoes, eggplant, sweet potatoes, and peaches.

**Recommendations:**
- Natural anti-bacterial agents
- Probiotics (30-50 billion CFUs) including Lactobacillus rhamnosus

### High HPHPA (Clostridia Marker)
HPHPA is an abnormal phenylalanine metabolite produced when byproducts of Clostridium bacteria combine with human metabolites. High concentrations cause abnormal behavior by inhibiting metabolism of dopamine to epinephrine, resulting in high levels of the dopamine metabolite HVA and insufficient epinephrine/norepinephrine.

**Associated conditions:**
- Behavioral, gastrointestinal, and neuropsychiatric symptoms
- Tic disorders, depression, autism, schizophrenia, aggression
- Seizures, anorexia, obsessive compulsive disorder, hyperactivity

*Note: Neuropsychiatric effects are more common when values exceed 500 mmol/mol creatinine (patient value: 471)*

**Clostridia species producing HPHPA:** C. sporogenes, C. caloritolerans, C. botulinum, C. mangenoti, C. ghoni, C. bifermentans, C. caproicum, C. sordellii

**Recommendations:**
- Treatment with Metronidazole or Vancomycin is close to 100% effective at killing parent organisms but not spores
- At least three months of probiotic therapy after antimicrobial treatment (due to spore formation)
- Clostridia overgrowth may be controlled with Corebiotic, Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG (Culturelle), or Saccharomyces boulardii
- **AVOID phenylalanine or tyrosine supplements** due to possible conversion to HPHPA or other toxic byproducts

### Low Aconitic Acid
May be due to impaired function of the Krebs cycle, low dietary intake of citrate-containing foods (citrus fruits/juices), potassium deficiency, acidosis, chronic kidney failure, diabetes, hypoparathyroidism, or excessive muscle activity.

### Low Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)
Indicates less than optimum level of the antioxidant vitamin C. Individuals who consume large amounts of vitamin C can still have low values if the sample is taken 12 or more hours after intake.

**Recommendations:**
- Supplementation with buffered vitamin C taken 2 or 3 times a day
